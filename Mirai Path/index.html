日本留学・就職ガイド Japan Career Guide

An administrative blueprint. Discover scholarship pathways, student visa requirements, local living budgets, and essential pre-departure checklists.

Step 01: April - May

Official Circular Release

The Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT) launches the official application circular. Prepare your academic scripts, letters of recommendation, IELTS transcripts, and preliminary research abstracts.

Step 02: June - July

Primary Screening

The local Japanese Embassy reviews the documents. Shortlisted applicants undergo standard written screening tests (including rigorous English and Japanese tests) and a panel interview.

Step 03: August - September

University Acceptance

Passing Embassy candidates must actively contact professors in top Japanese national universities (e.g., Tokyo University, Tokyo Tech, Kyoto University) to secure a formal Letter of Provisional Acceptance.

Step 04: October - February

Secondary Screening

MEXT head offices in Tokyo perform the final review of recommended applicants. Upon positive selection, university placements and official award letters are dispatched.

Step 05: April

Departure to Japan

Secure the special student visa at your embassy, book MEXT-sponsored flights, and land in Japan to begin your research/language course!

Essential Documents

  • Academic Degree Certificates: Valid Bachelor transcripts and certificate translations.
  • Standardized English Scores: Verified IELTS (band 6.5+) or TOEFL certificates.
  • Research Proposal: A 2-page detailed technical breakdown of your graduate topic.
  • Recommendation Letters: Minimum of two references signed by professors or company tech leads.
  • Certificate of Eligibility (COE): Dispatched by your chosen Japanese school to activate visa steps.

Visa & Enrollment Steps

  • Contacting Professors: Find professors matching your exact field. Write a highly tailored email summarizing your research proposal and project portfolios.
  • Entrance Examinations: Preparing for university-specific math, programming, or physics entrance papers (often conducted in English).
  • Embassy Submission: Handing over the official COE, passport, photographs, and university acceptance letters to the Embassy of Japan.
  • Scholarship backup: In case MEXT is missed, prepare applications for private foundation scholarships (e.g., Rotary Yoneyama, Honjo Foundation).

Monthly Expenses (Est.)

  • Rent (Dormitory/Apartment): ¥35,000 - ¥70,000 (Tokyo averages are higher, regional campuses are very cost-effective).
  • Food & Groceries: ¥30,000 - ¥40,000 (cooking at home is extremely affordable).
  • Utilities (Gas, Water, Power): ¥8,000 - ¥12,000.
  • Mobile Data & High-speed Internet: ¥4,000 - ¥6,000.
  • National Health Insurance (NHI): ¥1,500 - ¥3,000 (huge student discount).
  • Local Transport: ¥5,000 - ¥10,000 (fully covered by student rail cards).

Financial Strategies

  • MEXT Allowance: MEXT researchers receive ¥143,000 - ¥145,000 per month, which fully covers tuition, housing, and savings!
  • Part-time Work (Arubaito): The student visa allows up to 28 hours of work per week. High-paying English teaching or tech freelancing yields ¥1,200 - ¥2,500 per hour.
  • TA & RA Positions: Serve as a Teaching Assistant or Research Assistant inside your laboratory for additional hourly university payouts.
